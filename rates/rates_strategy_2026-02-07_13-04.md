### **2. Commodity Outlook: Gold (XAU/USD)**

Gold is a non-yielding asset, making its valuation extremely sensitive to the opportunity cost of capital, proxied by real yields, and the strength of its primary denominating currency, the USD.

**Macro Driver Impact:** The expected rise in the 2Y UST yield combines with anchored long-term inflation expectations (suggested by the moderate 10Y yield at 4.331%) to drive U.S. **real yields higher**. Concurrently, the strengthening USD creates a significant headwind for the commodity.

**Outlook:** **BEARISH GOLD**

*   **Rationale (Opportunity Cost Spike):** The Fed’s success in anchoring inflation expectations while maintaining aggressively restrictive nominal rates implies a rising real cost of money. As the 2Y moves towards 5.00%, the attractiveness of risk-free cash/duration substitutes for Gold increases substantially, pressuring the yellow metal.
*   **USD Headwind:** Gold maintains a strong historical inverse correlation with the U.S. Dollar. Our expectation for a stronger USD (driven by yield divergence) adds structural selling pressure to XAU/USD.
*   **Strategy:** Initiate **Short Gold (XAU/USD)**. Maintain a defensive posture until there is explicit fundamental evidence that the Fed is softening its "Higher for Longer" commitment.
